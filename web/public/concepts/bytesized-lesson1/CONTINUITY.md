# Lesson 1 continuity contract

This file is the source of truth for future Taco Night scene revisions.

## Character locks

- Ant: girl, age 6; tight curly high top bun with pink band; pearl earrings; pink dress; blue high-top sneakers; cyan wristband.
- Lena: toddler girl, age 1½; much smaller than Ant; brown high ponytail with pink tie; pink footless onesie; barefoot.
- Mr. Goode: adult father; bald with defining scalp scar; gray T-shirt and red shorts during the store run.
- Mrs. Goode: adult mother; high bun; pink T-shirt; ripped cuffed jeans.

Mr. Goode is at the store from 10:18 AM through checkout. A split frame may show home and store simultaneously, but he must appear only on the store side. Phones appear only when a call or list action requires them. Characters otherwise look at and handle the food, cart, bags, or one another.

## Prop and shopping locks

- Cart: one gray steel grocery cart with a red handle.
- Bags: three reusable bags—navy, green, and red.
- Starting fridge: half jar of salsa, one lime, six tortillas, and nearly empty milk.
- Final grocery purchase: ground beef, soft tortillas, crunchy shells, lettuce, cheese, sour cream, avocados, milk, mild taco seasoning, and foil.
- Tomatoes never enter the store cart. Xavier's family supplies them.
- Groceries cost $42. Separate ice cream costs $5. Final budget is $13.

## Scene order

1. Mrs. Goode, Ant, and Lena check the fridge.
2. The parents establish the plan before Mr. Goode leaves.
3. Mr. Goode enters the store with an empty locked cart and folded bags.
4. Ground beef enters first; mild seasoning and foil follow only after the home updates.
5. Checkout contains the ten approved grocery items.
6. Ice cream is purchased separately.
7. The four Goodes prepare food; no tomatoes are visible before Xavier arrives.
8. Xavier and Nicky arrive; Xavier brings tomatoes.
9. All six diners appear at Taco Night.
